Recipe 1: Rice with Chicken

  • Time: 150 minutes
  • Serves: 3 days
Ingredients Quantity
Rice 1600 gr
Chicken 1 kg
Soy sauce 2 tablespoons
Salt for rice 2 tablespoons
Oil for rice 3 tablespoons
Onions 2 pieces
Carrot 2 pieces
Can of sweet corn 1

Steps (Recipe 1)

Step 1: Buy all ingredients.

Step 2: Clean onion and cut it into small pieces.

Step 3: Roast onions for 5 minutes, then add sweet corn and roast 3 minutes.

Step 4: Wash carrot and grate it, then add to corn and onions.

Step 5: Wash rice and add it to the pan.

Step 6: Add salt and oil for rice.

Step 7: Add water and cook everything together.

Step 8: Wash chicken, cut it and dry it.

Step 9: Add soy sauce, salt, and black pepper. Leave for 25–30 minutes, then cook chicken.

Conclusion (Recipe 1): This is tasty and filling food. Great if you are a student or living alone. You can eat it for 3–4 days and save time.

Recipe 2: Omelette Sandwich

  • Time: 10–15 minutes
  • Serves: 1–2
Ingredients Quantity
Eggs 2
Bread 2 slices
Cheese 2 slices
Tomato 1/2
Salt 1 pinch
Butter or oil 1 tablespoon

Steps (Recipe 2)

Step 1: Crack 2 eggs into a bowl, add salt, and mix.

Step 2: Heat a pan and add butter (or oil).

Step 3: Pour eggs into the pan and cook until set.

Step 4: Put cheese on the omelette, fold it, and cook 1 more minute.

Step 5: Toast bread, add tomato, then put omelette inside.

Conclusion (Recipe 2): Fast breakfast or dinner. Cheap, simple, and tasty.

Recipe 3: Pasta with Tuna

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Pasta 200–250 g
Canned tuna 1 can
Onion 1/2
Tomato sauce (or ketchup) 3–4 tablespoons
Salt to taste
Oil 1 tablespoon

Steps (Recipe 3)

Step 1: Boil pasta in salted water until ready.

Step 2: Cut onion and fry it in oil for 2–3 minutes.

Step 3: Add tuna and mix.

Step 4: Add tomato sauce and cook 2 minutes.

Step 5: Mix pasta with the sauce and serve.

Conclusion (Recipe 3): Simple “student” meal: quick, protein from tuna, and very filling.
